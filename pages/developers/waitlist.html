Developer economics

How selling through Nexus works.

Nexus is designed for builders who want their automation work to be sold as clear business outcomes, with the platform handling the buyer-facing setup, payment, execution, and dashboard layer.

How developers get paid

Buyer payments are collected through Nexus. Approved developer products earn from paid sales, and developer earnings are tracked inside the platform before payout.

Revenue share

The target standard model is a 20% Nexus platform fee after payment processing costs, with the developer receiving the remaining share. The first cohort may receive a reduced platform fee while Nexus fine-tunes the developer side.

Payout timeline

Early payouts may be reviewed and sent manually on a regular payout cycle. The goal is to keep payout status visible to developers as the wallet flow matures.

No upfront listing fee

There is no upfront listing fee during early access. Nexus only wants builders who can create useful, reliable automation outcomes for real buyers.

Founding developer badge

Early approved builders can be marked as founding developers, helping buyers understand who joined during the first cohort.

Priority review

Early developers get faster review on profiles and product submissions while the marketplace catalog is being shaped.

Influence the platform

The first cohort helps decide which developer-side tools matter most, including product submission, messaging, earnings, reviews, and support workflows.

Trust and ownership

Clear responsibilities for every automation.

Nexus separates the marketplace platform, the developer's workflow logic, and the buyer's setup inputs so each side knows what they own.

Nexus owns the platform layer

Nexus manages product pages, setup forms, credential flow, execution, monitoring, buyer dashboard, payment handling, order records, and the support path around the platform experience.

Developers own workflow logic

Developers own the automation logic they submit and any guided setup they offer. They are responsible for making the workflow understandable, reliable, and appropriate for the listed outcome.

Buyers own access and inputs

Buyers are responsible for accurate business inputs, correct account access, valid credentials, and the source data needed for the automation to run as expected.

FAQ

Developer waitlist questions.

Short answers for builders and buyers who want to understand what Nexus is, how the marketplace works, and what early developers can expect.

What is Nexus?

Nexus is a marketplace for practical business automation outcomes: ready-made workflows, AI agents, reports, operational tools, and custom automation requests.

How do developers join?

Developers join the waitlist, share the automation types they build and the stack they use, then Nexus reviews fit before opening early developer access.

How do developers earn?

Approved products can earn from paid sales through Nexus. Nexus applies the agreed platform fee and tracks developer earnings before payout.

What do buyers receive?

Buyers receive a clear listing, expected output preview, setup path, dashboard access, support route, and the automation output or workflow they purchased.

Can developers offer guided setup?

Yes. Developers can offer guided setup when a product needs human configuration, buyer-specific credentials, or process-specific setup support.

Is there an upfront listing fee?

No. During early access there is no upfront listing fee. Nexus is focused on finding strong builders and useful products first.

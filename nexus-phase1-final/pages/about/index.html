The problem

AI is useful. Buying AI automation is still confusing.

Most companies already know AI can help. The problem is not awareness. The problem is execution. Businesses do not want to spend weeks comparing tools, hiring developers, connecting APIs, managing workflow hosting, or trying to understand what is real and what is hype.

Today, if a company wants an automated report, an AI chatbot, social listening, lead enrichment, or a custom workflow, they usually have four bad options: build it internally, hire an agency, buy a tool that only solves part of the problem, or download a template they do not know how to maintain.

Setup paths

Self-serve when simple. Nexus guided install when complex.

One reason automation marketplaces fail is that buyers are left alone after purchase. Nexus is built around setup clarity from the beginning.

Self-Serve

For simple automations

The buyer can follow instructions, provide required details, connect accounts, and launch the automation with minimal help.

Best when:
  • The workflow is standardized.
  • The buyer has the required accounts ready.
  • The setup steps are simple and repeatable.
  • No deep custom integration is needed.
Nexus Guided Install

For higher-value or complex setups

Nexus helps prepare, configure, test, and launch the automation so the business does not have to manage the technical workflow alone.

Best when:
  • The automation connects multiple tools.
  • Business data or knowledge needs preparation.
  • The company wants a managed setup experience.
  • Reliability matters from the first launch.
The long-term vision

The long-term value is not only listing automations. The real value is building trust around what works. As more businesses use Nexus, the platform can collect reviews, performance signals, industry fit, setup difficulty, and buyer outcomes.
